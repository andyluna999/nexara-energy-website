# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Decision-makers:** Executives and operations leaders evaluating power infrastructure providers. They arrive skeptical, want a fast credibility read, and need to feel confident handing the coordination over to a single vendor. Context: deliberate B2B evaluation mode, often cross-referencing competitors.

**Technical evaluators:** Engineers validating feasibility, reliability, and integration fit. They scrutinize specs, want to see field experience, and are allergic to marketing fluff. They read the fine print.

Both arrive from outbound or referral — they're already interested but unconvinced. Low patience for visual noise.

## Product Purpose

Nexara's marketing website communicates the company's credibility as a single-interface power infrastructure provider for remote and mission-critical industrial operations. The site's job: convert skeptical evaluators into inquiry submitters by making Nexara feel technically rigorous, operationally experienced, and easy to coordinate with.

Success = a qualified prospect submitting the contact form or picking up the phone.

## Brand Personality

**Operationally confident. Technically precise. Refreshingly clear.**

Tone: like an experienced engineer who also knows how to run a business. Not corporate-consultant, not startup-casual. Straight-talking, field-earned, no buzzword bloat.

Reference: the gap between a PowerPoint slide deck (what Nexara has) and what a company like ABB or Siemens would put on a dedicated product landing page.

## Anti-references

- The current HOW diagram: hub-and-spoke with colored rounded boxes, icon circles, bulleted slides — feels like a PowerPoint export, not a website
- Generic energy consulting sites: stock images of power lines, slide-show carousels, corporate-speak copy
- Rounded-corner card grids with large icons above headings (template feel)
- SaaS-cream / startup-minimal (wrong register for industrial B2B)
- Any layout that would feel equally at home on a fintech landing page

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual element either carries information or creates structure. Nothing for aesthetics alone.
2. **Show the system, don't describe it.** The HOW section should make the integrated model feel visually self-evident — the layout IS the argument.
3. **Trust before sell.** Credibility signals (experience, specificity, field data) appear before marketing claims.
4. **Sharp and directional.** No soft edges. The diagonal motif mirrors how Nexara operates: cutting through complexity at an angle, not circling around it.
5. **Earned restraint.** Not minimal because minimal is safe — precise because the audience reads imprecision as incompetence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum
- Bilingual: EN/ES (all sections)
- Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations
- High readability priority: line length capped, contrast ratios checked
